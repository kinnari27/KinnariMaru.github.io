About

Comfortable being the person who makes new tech work.

I spend most of my time in Kotlin and Jetpack Compose, and just as much in Flutter and Dart — I don't think of myself as "Android" or "cross-platform," just as someone who picks the right stack for the problem. My actual job is closer to translation — turning a healthcare team's scheduling problem, or a transport company's live-tracking need, into an app that quietly does the right thing in the background. Redirect Health needed patients to trust their appointment data was current; Shivohm Cab Services needed drivers and riders to see the same trip status at the same moment. Both come down to the same discipline: clean architecture underneath, and real-time sync that doesn't drop a beat.

I also treat AI tools — Claude, Copilot, ChatGPT — the way a carpenter treats a new saw: not a shortcut, but a way to pick up an unfamiliar framework or API fast enough to ship on schedule. It's how someone with five years of native Android depth ends up equally comfortable shipping a Flutter app solo, end to end, on nights and weekends.
